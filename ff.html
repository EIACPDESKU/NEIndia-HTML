Biodiversity of North East India

Flora

Foxtail Orchid (Rhynchostylis retusa)

The state flower of Arunachal Pradesh, valued for its ornamental beauty and use in traditional medicines.

Blue Vanda (Vanda coerulea)

An endangered orchid species, prized for its striking blue flowers and use in hybridization.

Brahma Kamal (Saussurea obvallata)

A rare alpine flower with significant cultural and medicinal value, found in high-altitude areas of Sikkim and Arunachal Pradesh.

Agarwood (Aquilaria malaccensis)

Known for its fragrant resin, used in perfumes and incense; critically endangered due to overexploitation.

Rudraksha Tree (Elaeocarpus ganitrus)

Sacred in Hinduism, its seeds are used for making prayer beads.

Indian Gooseberry (Phyllanthus emblica)

Rich in vitamin C, it’s used in traditional medicine and supplements for its antioxidant properties.

Neem (Azadirachta indica)

A multipurpose medicinal tree known for its antimicrobial properties, widely used in Ayurveda.

Bamboo (Bambusoideae, various species)

Over 50 species found in the region; used for construction, crafts, and as food (bamboo shoots).

Indian Barberry (Berberis aristata)

Used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties.

Tea (Camellia sinensis)

Assam is famous for tea production, which supports the economy and provides global recognition.

Fauna

One-Horned Rhinoceros (Rhinoceros unicornis)

A flagship species for conservation in Assam, found in Kaziranga and Manas National Parks.

Hoolock Gibbon (Hoolock hoolock)

India’s only ape species, an indicator of healthy forest ecosystems.

Red Panda (Ailurus fulgens)

A vulnerable species found in Arunachal Pradesh and Sikkim, important for its role in the Himalayan ecosystem.

Golden Langur (Trachypithecus geei)

An endangered primate species, endemic to Assam and Bhutan, crucial for biodiversity conservation.

Indian Elephant (Elephas maximus indicus)

A keystone species that helps maintain forest ecosystems through seed dispersal.

Clouded Leopard (Neofelis nebulosa)

Vulnerable species and a top predator in the forest ecosystem of North East India.

Great Indian Hornbill (Buceros bicornis)

Known for its role in seed dispersal; culturally significant to indigenous tribes.

Snow Leopard (Panthera uncia)

An apex predator in high-altitude ecosystems, found in the eastern Himalayas.

Pygmy Hog (Porcula salvania)

The smallest wild pig species, critically endangered and found only in Assam.

Bengal Slow Loris (Nycticebus bengalensis)

A nocturnal primate, important for maintaining forest health as a pollinator and seed disperser.

Indian Pangolin (Manis crassicaudata)

Critically endangered due to poaching; helps control insect populations by feeding on ants and termites.

Golden Mahseer (Tor putitora)

An important fish species in rivers, valued for its role in aquatic ecosystems and as a game fish.

White-Winged Duck (Asarcornis scutulata)

Critically endangered, found in Assam’s wetlands, and vital for wetland biodiversity.

Giant River Otter (Pteronura brasiliensis)

An indicator species for freshwater ecosystems in the region.

Assam Roofed Turtle (Pangshura sylhetensis)

Endemic to North East India; plays a role in maintaining aquatic habitats.

Bar-Headed Goose (Anser indicus)

A high-altitude migratory bird, contributing to the ecological balance of wetlands.

King Cobra (Ophiophagus hannah)

The world’s longest venomous snake, plays a key role in controlling rodent populations.

Himalayan Monal (Lophophorus impejanus)

A colorful bird found in high-altitude regions, culturally significant and a state bird of Uttarakhand (also seen in Sikkim).

River Dolphin (Platanista gangetica)

Endangered freshwater dolphin species, found in the Brahmaputra; crucial for river ecosystems.

Flying Squirrel (Petaurista philippensis)

Found in forest canopies, contributes to seed dispersal and forest regeneration.
