Flora and Fauna

Tripura

Mammals:

  • Elephant (Elephas maximus): A large herbivore found in the forests of Tripura.
  • Sloth Bear (Melursus ursinus): Found in the region’s dense jungles.
  • Binturong (Arctictis binturong): A tree-dwelling carnivore also called a bearcat.
  • Wild Dog (Cuon alpinus): Known for their pack behavior and agility.
  • Porcupine (Artherurus assamensis): A rodent species with spiny defenses.
  • Barking Deer (Muntiacus muntjak): Small and agile, often found in the forests.
  • Sambar (Cervus unicolor): The largest deer species in India.
  • Wild Boar (Sus scrofa): A common inhabitant of forested areas.
  • Gaur (Bos gaurus): The Indian bison, known for its massive size.
  • Leopard (Panthera pardus): A large carnivorous feline.
  • Clouded Leopard (Neofelis nebulosa): A rare and elusive wild cat species.

Primates:

  • Hoolock Gibbon (Hoolock hoolock): A small ape with a distinctive call.
  • Slow Loris (Nycticebus bengalensis): A nocturnal primate.
  • Capped Langur (Trachypithecus pileatus): An arboreal primate species.
  • Phayre’s Langur (Trachypithecus phayrei): Endemic to the region and endangered.
  • Stump-Tailed Macaque (Macaca arctoides): A robust monkey species.
  • Pig-Tailed Macaque (Macaca leonina): Known for its distinctive short tail.

Birds:

Tripura hosts 341 bird species, including 58 migratory species, which enrich its avifauna diversity.

Butterflies:

High diversity of butterfly species documented across the state.

Aquatic Species:

289 aquatic flora and fauna species, including 78 fish species, are found in Tripura's water bodies.

Meghalaya

Flora:

  • Orchids (Various species): With 352 species, Meghalaya is a haven for orchid enthusiasts.
  • Primitive Flowering Plants (Various species): The state is termed the "Cradle of Flowering Plants."
  • Michelia lanuginosa: A flowering tree with soft, white blossoms.
  • Dendrobenthamia capitata: Known for its unique flowering patterns.
  • Nymphaea pygmaea: A small aquatic plant species with delicate flowers.
  • Schleichera trijuga: Used for medicinal and cosmetic purposes.
  • Sterculia (Sterculia spp.): Genus known for unique flowers and fruits.
  • Adinandra griffithii: An endemic and critically endangered plant.
  • Ceropegia arnottiana: Another endemic plant facing extinction.
  • Clematis apiculata: A vine-like plant with vibrant flowers.

Fauna:

  • Elephants (Elephas maximus): Commonly sighted in western and northwestern ranges.
  • Leopards (Panthera pardus and Neofelis nebulosa): Includes both the common and clouded leopards.
  • Jackals (Canis aureus): Found in the region's grasslands and forests.
  • Bears (Melursus ursinus): Part of Meghalaya's rich wildlife.
  • Various Deer Species (Various species): Includes multiple deer species across habitats.
  • Giant Squirrels (Ratufa spp.): Notable for their large size and vibrant coloration.
  • Reptiles (Various species): Includes vipers, cobras, pythons, and other serpents.
