Flora and Fauna

Assam

Mammals

  • Great One-Horned Rhinoceros (Rhinoceros unicornis): Iconic species of Kaziranga National Park.
  • Wild Water Buffalo (Bubalus arnee): Found in the floodplains of Assam.
  • Hog Deer (Axis porcinus): Commonly seen in grasslands.
  • Pygmy Hog (Porcula salvania): The world’s smallest wild pig species, critically endangered.
  • Hispid Hare (Caprolagus hispidus): A rare and elusive species of hare.
  • Golden Langur (Trachypithecus geei): Found in forests near the Bhutan border.
  • Golden Cat (Catopuma temminckii): A medium-sized wild cat found in Assam's forests.
  • Giant Civet (Viverra zibetha): The largest civet species found in India.
  • Binturong (Arctictis binturong): A tree-dwelling carnivore also known as a bearcat.
  • Hog Badger (Arctonyx collaris): A nocturnal mammal with a stocky build.
  • Porcupine (Hystrix indica): Rodents with spiny defenses.
  • Civet (Various species): Small carnivores commonly found in Assam.

Big Cats

  • Indian Tiger (Panthera tigris tigris): National animal of India, found in Assam’s protected areas.
  • Indian Leopard (Panthera pardus fusca): Found in Assam's forests and hills.
  • Clouded Leopard (Neofelis nebulosa): A rare and arboreal wild cat.
  • Black Panther (Panthera pardus): A melanistic form of the Indian leopard.

Deer Species

  • Swamp Deer (Rucervus duvaucelii duvaucelii): Known locally as Dolhorina, thrives in grasslands.
  • Chital (Axis axis): Also called Futukihorina, a spotted deer.
  • Sambar (Rusa unicolor): India’s largest deer species.
  • Indian Muntjac (Muntiacus muntjak): Also known as barking deer.
  • Wild Boar (Sus scrofa): Widely distributed across Assam.

Primates

  • Hoolock Gibbon (Hoolock hoolock): The only ape species found in India.
  • Assamese Macaque (Macaca assamensis): Native to the northeastern region.
  • Capped Langur (Trachypithecus pileatus): Arboreal species found in forests.
  • Golden Langur (Trachypithecus geei): Endangered primate species.

Reptiles

  • Reticulated Python (Malayopython reticulatus): One of the world’s largest snake species.
  • Rock Python (Python molurus): A large non-venomous snake.

Birds

  • Blue-Throated Barbet (Psilopogon asiaticus): Known locally as Hetuluka.
  • White-Winged Wood Duck (Asarcornis scultulata): Endangered, locally called Deuhnah.
  • Pallas’s Fish Eagle (Haliaeetus leucoryphus): Known as Kuruwa in Assam.
  • Great Pied Hornbill (Buceros bicornis): Locally called Rajdhonesh.
  • Himalayan Golden-Backed Three-Toed Woodpecker (Dinopium shorii): Locally known as Barhoituka.

Sikkim

Flora

  • Rhododendrons (Rhododendron spp.): The state boasts a stunning variety of species.
  • Orchids (Various species): With over 600 species, Sikkim is an orchid paradise.
  • Primulas (Primula spp.): Found in Sikkim’s colorful meadows.
  • Medicinal Herbs (Various species): Used in traditional medicine systems.

Fauna

  • Red Panda (Ailurus fulgens): The state animal of Sikkim, endangered and iconic.
  • Snow Leopard (Panthera uncia): Found in Sikkim’s high-altitude regions.
  • Himalayan Tahr (Hemitragus jemlahicus): A mountain goat with curved horns.
  • Musk Deer (Moschus spp.): Known for its scent glands, found in forested areas.

Birds: Sikkim hosts over 550 bird species, making it a birdwatcher’s haven.
